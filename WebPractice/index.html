Early Days In Seconday School

My interest for software development began when I was in year 7 when I was shown Scratch in I.T class. This was an appliction that allowed users to create 2D games or animations and was my first introduction into programming. Instead of writing lines of code it used blocks; which were basically variables and commands that could be dragged-and-dropped together to create functions or methods. This made it easy for first time programmers how programming worked, but in a very simplfied way.

University Days

I carried on my software development studies in university via Computer Science. The course gave a variety of subjects I had to study for such as Cryptography and Biocomputation. Both fun and interesting to learn, but struggled to stranslate the new informtion to code. One subject I enjoyed the most however was in my 4th year in university called Enterprise System Development (ESD). This subject was about learning how applications were made on an business/enterprise level. This was where i got most of my software development knowledge and skill. In this class I got back to using python but this time had got to learn how to use REACT.js. One of the assignements was to create a application for a made-up cinima company; not going to get into too much details, but the jist was that, a team of 5 would create a booking system that would allow customers to book seats for one or more people for a movie, have employees with specific amount of control over the system and customers, and have the admin with the most amount of contol over the system, other employees and customers.

Though this was a fun assignment me and my team didn't get to finish the product on time, therefore had to present with a system still full of bugs. Dispite this low moment, me and my team passed the class, as the assignmet was graded by the amount of work the individual in that team contributed to building the system, as well as the amount of objectives we managed to complete; I was lucky enough to have a team which all members participated in and completed enough objectives allowing us to pass the class.
